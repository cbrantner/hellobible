Our Partnership with the Kilgoris Project

Teach your child the importance of giving. Together we can make an impact!

We started an awesome partnership with the Kilgoris Project to support their great work in Kenya. The Kilgoris Project educates and feeds children of a Masaai village in southwest Kenya. They work with local leaders and families to operate schools, provide daily food and clean water.

The Kilgoris schools

The Kilgoris Project teachers and staff nourish the hearts, minds and bodies of more than 1,100 children every day. That impact stretches beyond the classroom into family life. Many students are the first in their families to attend school.

Preschools

Just like in the U.S., preschool students in Kenya love to learn letters and numbers, play outside, and sing songs with their teachers.

At the five preschools run by The Kilgoris Project, in rural Kenya, children as young as three have access to loving teachers and the tools they need to begin their educational journey. They listen to their teachers read their favorite books, use flash cards to recognize letters, trace letters and shapes with markers, and arrange objects in patterns all to build the literacy skills they will need in the future.

Kindergarten

The Kilgoris Project has a new batch of Kindergarteners, and in Kenya they are known as "top class" or the oldest class in preschool. "Top class" loves to read, draw, sing, and learn their letters. They also join the big kids for breakfast and lunch at school and playground time.

Graduation in Kilgoris

This month, we’re introducing you to one of the very first graduates of Intimigom Primary School – a Kilgoris Project school: Graduates Cynthia and Soila with the aunt celebrating graduation.

In fall 2016, 22 Intimigom students wore red graduation robes and celebratory tinsel for their graduation ceremony. Many are the first in their family to graduate primary school (8th grade) and are setting an example for their younger brothers and sisters.

Clean Water in Kilgoris

For kids in rural Kenya, clean water goes hand-in-hand with learning. Without clean water and proper nutrition, students simply can’t learn.

At schools run by The Kilgoris Project in rural Kenya, wells and rain-water collection systems help ensure students and teachers have access to clean water. Access to clean water also helps keep TKP’s seven school clean and safe. Teachers, staff and students are trained on proper hand-washing and food and water handling to help decrease the spread of illness and disease in the community.

Outside of school

Just like you, kids in rural Kenya have lots of interests and responsibilities outside of school. Students get up early in the morning to help their parents with chores like feeding animals, preparing tea and breakfast, and caring for younger siblings. They also get a chance to explore and develop their talents in art, dance, poetry and soccer activities:

Cultural Dance

One of the most popular activities at The Kilgoris Project rural Kenya is cultural dance. Girls of all ages gather with their teachers and practice dances and songs that highlight their Kenyan heritage.

Soccer

Do you like to play soccer? Kids in Kilgoris, Kenya love togames and sports, especially soccer. The Kilgoris Project recently partnered with the local community to support a soccer tournament drawing hundreds of players and spectators.

Scouting

Just like many young children in the United States, students Kenya participate in scouting. They learn teamwork, leadership, camping and survival skills. The boys in this program show their leadership abilities on campus and build their skills at local scouting competitions. TKP's team even advanced to a National-level competition last spring!
